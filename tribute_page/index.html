Mother Teresa

One of the Great Humanitarians of the 20th Century

Mother Teresa, also known as "Blessed Teresa of Calcutta", founded the Missionaries of Charity, and was deeply involved with treating the ill and helping the poor.

Here's a Timeline of Mother Teresa's life:

  • 1910: Born Anjeze Gonxhe Bojaxhiu in present-day Skopje, Republic of Macedonia.
  • 1919: Her father dies
  • 1922: At the age of 12, commits herself to a religious life.
  • 1928: Leaves home to join the Sisters of Loreto at Loreto Abbey in Rathfarnham, Ireland
  • 1929: Arrives in India to begin her novitiate in Darjeeling.
  • 1931: Takes her first religious vow as a nun on May 24th. Teresa is named after Thérèse de Lisieux, the patron saint of missionaries.
  • 1937: Takes a solemn vow on May 14th.
  • 1944: Appointed headmistress at the Loreto convent school in Entally, Calcutta.
  • 1948: Begins her missionary work with the poor.
  • 1950: Begins the Missionaries of the Charity with the Vatican Permission. It will grow from 13 initial nuns to over 4000 by 1997.
  • 1952: Opens the first Home for the Dying in Calcutta
  • 1963: The Missionaries of Charity Brothers is founded. A branch for the Sisters follow in 1976.
  • 1982: Rescues 37 children trapped in a front line hospital by brokering a cease-fire between Israel and Palestine forces.
  • 1983: Suffers her first heart attack in Rome while visiting Pope John Paul II
  • 1988: Visits Armenia following the earthquake to expand her efforts to Communist countries.
  • 1989: Receives an artificial pacemaker after a second heart attack
  • 1991: Returns to her homeland in Albania to open a Missionaries of Charity Brothers home. Contracts pneumonia while in Mexico, suffering further heart problems along the way.
  • 1996: Falls and breaks her collar bone in April. Suffers from Malaria and failure of the left heart ventricle in August.
  • 1997: Steps down from the head of Missionaries of Charity in March. Dies on September 5th.

"[Mother Teresa] was a rare and unique individual who lived long for higher purposes. Her life-long devotion to the care of the poor, the sick, and the disadvantaged was one of the highest examples of service to our humanity."

Nawaz Sharif, the Prime Minister of Pakistan

"She is the United Nations. She is peace in the world."

Javier Peerez de Cuellar, Former U.N. Secretary-General
